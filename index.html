Vincent van Gogh



life in color

I dream my painting,
and I paint my dream


Vincent van Gogh's paintings
are known and exhibited worldwide.
He started painting around the age of 27,
yet because of his 'fractured brain',
he took his life at the age of 37.

He made over 800 paintings, which were particularly celebrated postmortem.
Thanks to the letters he wrote to his loved ones,
there is a lot known about his life.

This website gives you new insights about Vincent van Gogh,
by visualizing his stories, quoting his thoughts and discovering his

life in color.

  • Discover more about young Vincent

    1853

    Vincent van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853. After he finished boarding school, he dropped out of secondary school in 1867.

  • His first years in the world of art

    1869

    At the age of 16, Vincent was offered a job by his uncle at an international art dealer. He started his lifelong correspondece with his brother Theo in 1872.

  • More about his search for direction

    1875

    After working in London, he was transferred to Paris, where he became religious. He searched for a meaning, and decided to serve God as an artist.

  • Vincent & Theo

    1880

    It was Vincent's brother Theo who advised him to focus on his art. Theo, whom still worked at their uncle's company, would support Vincent financially.

  • The first years as a painter

    1883

    Vincent moved back to his parents in 1881, but left the same year to take drawing lessons in The Hague. After a difficult affair with a woman, he moved back to his parents, again, this time in Nuenen.

  • Becoming Vincent

    1886

    After short and disenchanting experience at Antwerp's art academy, he went to Paris to take drawing classes. Here, Vincent was introduced to the work of Claude Monet. Vincent's paintings became brighter in this period.

  • Discover more about his Southern dream

    1889

    Vincent left overcrowded Paris to find peace in the South of France. It was his dream to live together with other artists. In 1888, he moved in with Paul Gauguin. The clash of their perspectives on painting drove Vincent mad, however. He took his life in 1890.

Conscience is a man's compass


As an impressionist, Vincent van Gogh aimed
to paint directly what he saw. Setting, sceneries, and colors
differ between the seasons in which he painted.
From over 630 of Vincent's artworks, the season in which
they were made can be stated based on the date.

From his time in Paris, however,
little of the letters he wrote to Theo remain,
which makes tracing dates of his paintings difficult.

Seasonality of work

In his initial years as an artist, Vincent was still finding his own style and technique. The way his artistic identity developed through time is influenced by the places in which he lived.

Whereas he painted sober households and sceneries in Nuenen around 1883-1885, he painted soft landscapes in the last years of his life, 1889-1890.

If you truly love nature
you will find beauty everywhere

I put my heart and my soul into my work,
and I have lost my mind in the process


Vincent started to show signs of confusion in Arles,
as living together with artist Paul Gauguin caused
them to have heated discussions about their work.
Vincent cut off his ear, and was hospitalized.

However, throughout his artistic journey,
his lust for life, nature, people and art
remained ever present.

The artist always got back behind the canvas.
